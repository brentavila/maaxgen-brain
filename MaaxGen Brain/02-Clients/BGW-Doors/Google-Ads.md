- Which products have high spend but no conversions?

- Are strong products being limited by poor feed data, poor images, weak titles, or low demand?
- Should products be segmented into different asset groups or campaigns?

## Known PMax Problem

A known issue with Performance Max Shopping is that Google may heavily favor a small number of products, giving them most of the impressions and clicks while other products receive very limited visibility.

For BGW Doors, we need to monitor product concentration closely.

## Product Concentration Analysis

When reviewing product data, check:

- Top 10 products by impressions
- Top 10 products by clicks
- Top 10 products by cost
- Top 10 products by conversion value
- Top 10 products by ROAS
- Products with spend but no conversions
- Products with high CTR but low conversion rate
- Products with low CTR but high conversion rate
- Products with strong revenue but limited impressions
- Products with poor feed quality
- Products with poor images
- Products with weak titles
- Products with unclear pricing or shipping expectations

## Location Action Options

Possible actions after reviewing location performance:

- Exclude consistently poor-performing cities or regions
- Separate strong regions into their own campaign if volume and budget justify it
- Separate California vs non-California if performance differs meaningfully
- Adjust location targeting settings
- Review whether traffic is coming from physical location or location intent
- Build city/state-specific landing page insights for SEO
- Document high-performing markets for future campaigns

## Important Note

For Performance Max, do not assume city-level bid adjustments are the main lever. Review location performance, then consider exclusions, campaign segmentation, targeting changes, or budget separation where appropriate.
